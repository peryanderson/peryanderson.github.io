Responsive Design

Responsive design is simply, design to the user. It involves several different facets, such as reformatting the information on a site based on what kind of device it is being used. The philosophy of responsive design is that a site should respond to how the user interacts with it. Here are some of the sources that we found useful.

Fluid Layouts

Fluid layouts are layouts that are based on fluid girds, that is the size of particular sections are defined as percentages of the screen that they occupy, rather than pixel lengths. This allows for resizing and responsive design. Fluid layouts do not on their own constitue representative design, but they are an important element of it.

Responsive design has become increasingly important due to the greatly increased types of screens that people use - from the smallest cell phone screens to the largest 4k monitors. Responsive design isn't always the best approach - directing users to alternate mobile sites is also a viable option. Responsive design and fluid layouts give a lot of flexibility, but make it difficult from a graphic design perspective. Pixel accurate image design becomes difficult to achieve when having to plan for so many different layouts.
